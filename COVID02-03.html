Good News from China. Not so much from other places.

As it is well known, the covid-19 started this january and emerged from the Hubei region in China. Now for the first time in the last couple of weeks the data is giving us reason to reason to believe that it is starting to fade, in China at least.

In this article I will delve into the data provided by Rami Krispin, in order to try to make sense of this. Furthermore, I will use a Kaggle dataset, in hopes of putting this into perspective.

Let’s start.

load("F:/Escritorio/Bases de datos Daniel/Carper/CoronavirusenColombia_files/coronavirus.rda")

You can download Rami Krispin’s data from his GitHub.

c1 <- coronavirus %>%
  select(date,type,cases,Country.Region) %>%
  group_by(date,type,Country.Region) %>%
  summarise(total_cases = sum(cases)) 

c2 <- as.data.frame(c1[which(c1$type=="confirmed" & c1$Country.Region == "Mainland China"),])

c2$day <- (1:length(c2$total_cases))

c2$corcumtotal_cases <- cumsum(c2$total_cases)
PlotChinalin <- ggplot(c2, aes(day,corcumtotal_cases)) + geom_line(size=1.25,color="red1") + 
  
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 12, face = "bold",hjust = 0.5),
    plot.caption = element_text(size = 8, face = "italic"),
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(), 
    panel.grid.major = element_line(color = "gray50", size = 0.5),
    panel.grid.major.x = element_blank(),
    panel.background = element_blank(),
    line = element_blank(),
    axis.ticks.length = unit(.15, "cm"),
    axis.ticks.y = element_blank(),
    axis.title.x = element_text(color="black", 
    size=12),
    axis.title.y = element_text(color="black",
    size=10,
    face="italic"))+
  
  scale_y_continuous(expand = c(0, 0), 
    limits=c(0.0,81000),                  
    breaks=seq(0.0,90000,10000),
    name = "Number of subjects")+
  
  scale_x_continuous(expand = c(0, 0),
    breaks=seq(0.0,40,5),
    name = "Days")+
  
  labs(title = "Number of confirmed cases China", 
       caption = "Info taken from RamiKrispin. ")

As you can see, the plot shows on a logarithmic scale that the rate of confirmed cases increase in the last two weeks is slowing down. Great news for China!

However, there are countries where the covid-19 is getting in a foothold, we will examine the data.

When looking at the graphs South Korea is a whole different story, it started a little later than China but has picked up pace in the past couple of weeks. However do keep in mind the scale of both graphs, in China we were looking at a graph that went all the way to 80,000 while in Korea we are looking at one that barely breaks the 4,000 figure.

One good thing to note about South Korea, is that yesterday (01-31) they reported far less confirmed cases than the day before (02-29). It is something to look out for.
